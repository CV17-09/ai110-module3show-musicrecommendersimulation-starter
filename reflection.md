# Reflection: Profile Comparisons

## High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi  
The High-Energy Pop profile recommends fast, upbeat songs with high energy and danceability, while the Chill Lofi profile shifts toward slower, more relaxed songs with lower energy and higher acousticness. This makes sense because the two profiles are looking for completely different listening experiences—one for excitement and one for focus or relaxation.

## High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock  
Both profiles prefer high-energy songs, but the genres are different. The pop profile recommends more upbeat and positive songs, while the rock profile leans toward heavier and more intense tracks like "Storm Runner." This shows that genre still plays an important role in shaping recommendations even when energy levels are similar.

## Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock  
These profiles produce very different results. Chill Lofi recommends calm, slow-paced songs, while Deep Intense Rock recommends loud and fast songs. This difference makes sense because their target energy and mood preferences are almost opposite.

## Edge Case (High Energy but Sad Mood)  
This profile produced interesting results because it combines conflicting preferences. Some songs were selected mainly because they matched the high energy requirement, even if the mood did not fully match. This shows that the model may prioritize certain features, like energy, over others, which can lead to unexpected recommendations.

## Why "Gym Hero" Appears Often  
"Gym Hero" appears in multiple profiles because it has very high energy and strong alignment with numeric features like tempo and danceability. Even if the genre or mood is not a perfect match, its high energy score gives it an advantage. This shows how the scoring system can favor certain songs when specific features are weighted more heavily.
